Studying unit 5 was quite different from the other units. Here instead of learning about the **achievement** that people have made in the ‘Computer Science’ field, we studied various **loop holes** such as **inequality, injustice, biases, societal hierarchy and strata** that exist in these fields- through thought provoking presentations.

All the presentations were unique in the sense that every group tried to raise critical questions and tried their best to answer them. However I personally feel group 4 presentation was quite interesting, which was about '**coding against odds**'. Which highlights the women's **experience** and **representation** in the Computer Science field. 

The reason behind choosing this presentation is that-it tried to clearly highlight 2 distinct problems that pull women from entering the CS field namely **‘societal stigmas’** and **‘psychological mindset**’. 

Something that I really found interesting and what aligned with my thought is how they started the presentation with the strong examples of women **‘Grace Hooper’** and **‘Ada Lovelace**’ and highlighted the points that women are the pioneers of coding and contributed significantly towards building codes and compilers. Later brought the topic into light that how women were part of the CS field in earlier eras and how today they are underpresentation because of societal stigmas like gender inequality and psychological issues like ‘imposter syndrome' where women doubt their ability and avoid taking risks.

Moreover, to explain in one line they talked about underrepresentation of women in the CS field?

However, I personally feel that the presentation would have been more impactful if they would have drawn the difference through comparing with the number of women which are currently there in the CS field than what was before. This tactic would have ensured the positive ending highlighting how there has been a significant shift in society's mindset as the number of girls in higher education has increased and how women have overcome their fear by tackling the risks.  Moreover they would have ended with a positive note and example  about how women's representation had brought **creativity** in the CS field.
